// OVERVIEW

The vigilant "skyward scouts" of the Nation's expanding frontier. Developed for long-range planetary reconnaissance and atmospheric surveying, Mauersegler units are equipped with an integrated LiDAR scanner and modular flight systems for rapid terrain documentation. Their mechanical wing assemblies and jet-assisted propulsion allow traversal of hostile environments inaccessible to ground units.

In newly established colonies, MSGR units are deployed ahead of all other personnel to chart terrain, assess structural stability, and secure landing zones. In the early stages of colonial development, they serve as the Nation's eyes in unfamiliar skies.

// BASIC OPERATIONAL INFORMATION

Primary purpose: exploration and documentation of newly found planetary surfaces using the integrated chest-mounted LiDAR system, which generates a 3D surface file for later mapping. Units can independently identify chemical elements and mineral formations via built-in sensors, allowing on-the-spot sampling decisions where weight preservation is critical.

Secondary roles include: anomaly scouting missions and physical transport of classified documents not suitable for radio transmission.

// TECHNICAL — SKELETON & BIOLOGY

Skeleton: titanium-reinforced carbon fiber, modeled on avian hollow bone architecture. Interior: polyethylene with air pockets, wrapped in carbon fiber sheet, encased in thin titanium layer.

Biological components are standard Replika-spec. Metabolic system is adapted for minimal energy consumption — near-total caloric absorption allows the unit to sustain extended flight on infrequent feeding cycles.

Body plating: sparse carbon fiber, no armor, no paint. Natural color: matte black/grey.

// TECHNICAL — WING & PROPULSION MODULE

Wings modeled after glider planes and the common swift — designed for maximum aerodynamic efficiency with minimal propulsion demand. Small jet engines run on hydrogen fuel (low mass, high energy output).

Landing & takeoff: lower leg bones serve as pneumatic shock absorbers (titanium tube dual-chamber: oil + nitrogen). Pressure and absorption adjustable mid-flight. Wearable jumping stilts with curved springs aid takeoff and bear claw attachments for surface grip.
